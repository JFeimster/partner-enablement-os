# When the Bank Has to Say “Not Right Now,” Give the Client a Next Step

Some business owners are not bad borrowers.

They are just not bankable **today**.

Maybe they are too new.  
Maybe collateral is thin.  
Maybe credit is bruised.  
Maybe revenue is real but documentation is messy.  
Maybe the bank process is too slow for the opportunity in front of them.

A decline or delay does not have to become a dead relationship.

Darwin Hanneman and Moonshine Capital help business owners explore funding options when traditional bank financing is delayed, unavailable, or not the right fit today.

You can:

## Good Referral Situations

Refer a business client who needs help with:

- payroll, inventory, materials, or vendor payments
- equipment, truck, trailer, or machinery needs
- urgent repairs or short-term working capital
- unpaid invoices or receivables
- startup or early-stage business capital
- expansion, contracts, or growth funding
- a next step after a bank decline or delay

---

## Capital Options That May Help

### Fast Working Capital: Up to $25K for Urgent Business Needs

For active businesses that need fast capital for payroll, repairs, materials, inventory, insurance, or short-term cash-flow gaps.

**Best fit:** when the business need is real and the bank timeline cannot meet the urgency.

---

### Business Line Access: Working Capital Without the Weekly Fire Drill

For established businesses that need flexible access to working capital over time instead of constantly scrambling for one-time cash.

**Best fit:** when the client needs a financial shock absorber, not just a one-time bandage.

---

### Equipment & Truck Financing: Keep Cash in the Business

For trucks, trailers, machinery, work vehicles, tools, or revenue-producing equipment.

**Best fit:** when the asset supports the business but the bank cannot move fast enough or the file does not fit today.

---

### Larger Funding Reviews: Up to $2M for Qualified Businesses

For stronger businesses with revenue, documentation, and larger capital needs tied to expansion, equipment, contracts, working capital, refinancing, or growth.

**Best fit:** when the business has enough strength to support a bigger review, but bank financing is not the current fit.

---

## Best-Fit Referrals Usually Have

- an active business or financeable startup profile
- business revenue, income, collateral, receivables, or another realistic repayment source
- a real use for funds
- a funding need the bank cannot support today
- willingness to complete a short application

Not ideal for people with no active business, no repayment path, no clear use of funds, or expectations of guaranteed approval.
