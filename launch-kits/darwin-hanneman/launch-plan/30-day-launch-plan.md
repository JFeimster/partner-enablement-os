# Darwin Hanneman  
## 30-Day Funding Partner Launch Plan  
**Moonshine Capital Affiliate Launch Kit | Deliverable 1**

## Mission

Darwin’s first 30 days are not about building a giant brand, becoming a funding encyclopedia, or trying to explain every product under the sun.

The mission is simpler:

**Start real conversations with referral partners who already know business owners with funding problems.**

Darwin’s advantage is relationship credibility. He can walk into a bank, equipment dealer, accountant’s office, or contractor relationship and sound like a real human being — not a loan-bro NPC yelling “same-day funding” into the void.

The call’s strategy was clear: Darwin should operate as a **relationship-first commercial funding connector** backed by Moonshine Capital’s broader funding ecosystem, using one application to help route business owners toward a best-fit funding path instead of forcing every lead into one provider’s box. The Fathom summary specifically frames the model as a clearinghouse using an AI-driven application to match clients with the “best yes,” and identifies bank managers, accountants, and attorneys as primary referral targets. fileciteturn1file1

# Positioning Statement

**Darwin helps business owners explore commercial funding options through one simple application, backed by Moonshine Capital’s broader funding partner network. He focuses on relationship-first referrals from banks, accountants, attorneys, equipment dealers, trucking contacts, and construction-related businesses.**

Cleaner public-facing version:

> **I help business owners explore practical funding options when banks are slow, traditional financing does not fit, or cash flow becomes the bottleneck. One short application helps identify possible funding paths based on the business profile.**

Bank/referral partner version:

> **When you have a business client you cannot fund, I can give them a responsible next step without making you send them into random online lending chaos.**

Blue-collar/trucking/equipment version:

> **When equipment, repairs, payroll, or job cash flow becomes the problem, I help business owners see what funding options may be available before the opportunity disappears.**

# Weekly Sprint Breakdown

## Week 1 — Setup, Positioning, and First Conversations

### Objective

Get Darwin’s basic launch assets ready and begin low-pressure outreach.

### Primary focus

- Finalize public-facing identity
- Confirm LLC / brand verbiage
- Confirm application link
- Create QR code
- Draft simple business card copy
- Build first referral target list
- Start warm conversations

### Weekly targets

| Activity | Target |
|---|---:|
| Referral partner list built | 50 names |
| Warm contacts identified | 25 names |
| Referral conversations | 10 |
| Business owner conversations | 5 |
| Applications submitted | 1–2 |
| Follow-ups completed | 10 |

### Referral partner targets for Week 1

Start with people easiest to approach:

1. Bank managers Darwin already knows or can reasonably reach
2. Equipment contacts
3. Existing business relationships
4. Accountants used by local business owners
5. Attorneys who help with LLCs or business formation
6. Trucking/construction operators in Darwin’s network

### Daily activity targets

| Daily Action | Minimum |
|---|---:|
| New referral partner names added | 10 |
| Direct outreach messages/calls | 5 |
| Real conversations | 2 |
| Follow-ups | 2 |
| Notes logged | Same day |

### What to say

**Simple opener:**

> “I’m building a commercial funding referral resource for business owners who may not fit traditional bank financing or need faster working capital options. I’m not trying to replace the bank or advisor relationship. I’m trying to give people a practical next step when funding becomes the bottleneck.”

**For banks:**

> “When you have a business client you cannot fund, I may be able to help them explore alternative funding options. If nothing fits, we’ll tell them that. But at least they are not left wandering around the internet clicking on financial landmines.”

**For equipment/trucking/construction:**

> “If a business owner needs equipment, repairs, job startup cash, or bridge working capital, I can help them submit one short application and see what options may be available.”

### What to avoid

- Do not say “everyone qualifies.”
- Do not promise same-day funding as a universal outcome.
- Do not pitch every funding product.
- Do not lead with DAC, Credit Suite, or any single provider.
- Do not over-explain the backend.
- Do not spend the whole week designing a logo like it’s the Sistine Chapel of loan brokering.
- Do not join paid chambers before revenue or traction.

### Week 1 win condition

Darwin has a working application link/QR code, a starter list of 50 referral prospects, and at least 10 real conversations started.

### Referral partner targets for Week 2

Prioritize:

1. Community bank branch managers
2. Business bankers
3. Credit unions with business accounts
4. Local CPAs
5. Bookkeepers serving small businesses
6. Business formation attorneys
7. Small business attorneys

### Daily activity targets

| Daily Action | Minimum |
|---|---:|
| Bank/accountant/attorney outreach | 5 |
| Equipment/trucking/construction outreach | 2 |
| Follow-ups | 4 |
| Real conversations | 3 |
| CRM/tracker updates | Daily |

### What to say

**Bank manager walk-in / call opener:**

> “I work with business owners who may not fit traditional bank underwriting but still need a responsible next step. I’m looking to build relationships with bankers who occasionally have clients they cannot fund but still want to help.”

**Accountant opener:**

> “Your clients probably ask money questions before they ask lenders. I’m building a resource for business owners who need working capital, equipment funds, or funding options after a bank decline. I’d like to be a resource when the topic comes up.”

**Attorney opener:**

> “When your clients form, buy, or expand businesses, funding often becomes the next obstacle. I help them explore practical funding paths without you having to become their lender.”

### What to avoid

- Do not ask for referrals before establishing trust.
- Do not make the bank look bad.
- Do not say “we approve what banks deny.”
- Do not discuss referral compensation casually unless structure is clear and compliant.
- Do not bury people in product details.
- Do not sound desperate. Hungry is good. Desperate smells like expired shrimp.

### Week 2 win condition

Darwin identifies at least **one serious referral partner candidate** and submits at least **2–3 total applications** from early conversations.

### Weekly targets

### Referral partner targets for Week 3

Double down on whichever lane shows the most response from Weeks 1–2:

- If banks respond: focus banks.
- If equipment contacts respond: focus equipment.
- If trucking/construction responds: focus blue-collar funding use cases.
- If accountants respond: build advisor referral process.

### Daily activity targets

| Daily Action | Minimum |
|---|---:|
| New outreach | 5 |
| Follow-ups | 5 |
| Real conversations | 3 |
| Application asks | 2 |
| Referral source check-ins | 2 |
| Notes logged | Daily |

### What to say

**To referral partners after initial contact:**

> “I wanted to circle back. The easiest way to think of me is as a next-step funding resource. If you have a business owner who does not fit your box, I can help them explore options through one short application and see if there is a realistic path.”

**To business owners:**

> “The first step is not a hard pitch. It’s just getting enough basic information to see what funding paths may fit. If nothing makes sense, we say that. If something does, you can decide whether it is worth pursuing.”

**To equipment/trucking/construction contacts:**

> “If cash flow is holding up a repair, equipment purchase, payroll, materials, or a job start, send them through the short application. We’ll see what options may be available based on their business profile.”

### What to avoid

- Do not keep chasing dead conversations forever.
- Do not treat every “maybe” like a pipeline.
- Do not confuse activity with progress.
- Do not celebrate vanity conversations that never move to next step.
- Do not take rejection personally. The market is giving data, not writing your obituary.

### Week 3 win condition

Darwin has a clear sense of which 1–2 referral lanes deserve more attention and has **5–10 total applications submitted or in motion**.

# Referral Partner Targets

## Tier 1: Highest Priority

### Bank Managers / Business Bankers

Best because they already have business owners asking for money.

**Good referral situations:**

- bank declined the client
- client lacks collateral
- credit is below bank threshold
- short time in business
- revenue is inconsistent
- needs fast working capital
- needs equipment or repair money
- bank timeline is too slow

**Darwin’s angle:**

> “I help protect the relationship by giving declined clients a next step.”

---

### Accountants / Bookkeepers

Best because they see financial stress early.

**Good referral situations:**

- tax bill pressure
- payroll gaps
- expansion costs
- inventory/material purchases
- cash-flow crunch
- slow receivables
- client wants to grow but lacks capital

**Darwin’s angle:**

> “Your client already trusts you. I can be the funding resource you point them to when money becomes the obstacle.”

---

### Small Business Attorneys

Best because they work with businesses during formation, acquisition, expansion, partnership changes, and contract issues.

**Good referral situations:**

- new business formation
- acquisition or buyout
- equipment need
- working capital issue
- contract mobilization
- expansion
- bank decline

**Darwin’s angle:**

> “When capital becomes the next business problem, I can help your client explore options.”

---

## Tier 2: Strong Niche Targets

### Equipment Dealers

**Good referral situations:**

- customer wants equipment but financing stalls
- repair cost blocks revenue
- owner needs working capital plus equipment
- bank approval timeline is too slow

**Darwin’s angle:**

> “When financing is the bottleneck, I give your customer another path to explore.”

---

### Trucking Operators / Repair Shops

**Good referral situations:**

- truck repair
- engine/transmission issue
- fuel cash flow
- insurance/payment timing
- owner-operator expansion
- slow-paying loads

**Darwin’s angle:**

> “If the truck is down, the revenue is down. Let’s see what options may be available.”

---

### Contractors / Construction Businesses

**Good referral situations:**

- materials
- payroll
- job mobilization
- equipment rental
- bridge cash flow
- delayed receivables
- project expansion

**Darwin’s angle:**

> “If cash flow is holding up the job, let’s explore funding options before the opportunity disappears.”

# First Funding Application Goal

## 30-Day Goal

**5–10 submitted applications**

That is the realistic target for a new launch without pretending the money tree blooms by motivational sunshine.

## Minimum acceptable goal

**3 submitted applications**

If Darwin gets fewer than 3 applications in 30 days, the review should focus on:

- not enough conversations
- weak CTA
- unclear target niche
- too much explaining
- poor follow-up
- wrong audience
- no direct ask

## Strong goal

**10+ submitted applications**

If Darwin hits 10+ applications, Week 5 should focus on:

- quality of applicants
- which source produced them
- approval/funding movement
- referral partner repeatability
- faster follow-up system

# First Referral Partner Goal

## 30-Day Goal

**Secure 1 serious referral partner who understands when to send a business owner to Darwin.**

Not “liked the idea.”  
Not “said sounds good.”  
Not “we should totally connect sometime,” which is business-speak for “may this conversation vanish into the fog.”

A serious referral partner means:

- they understand who to refer
- they know the application link/QR code
- they have Darwin’s contact info
- they are open to sending a client when the situation appears
- Darwin has a follow-up date with them

## Stretch goal

**3–5 referral partners worth nurturing**

These do not all need to send leads immediately. They need to be real enough to justify follow-up.

# What Darwin Should Say More Often

## The best core line

> “I’m not here to replace your bank, accountant, attorney, or advisor. I’m here to give business owners a practical next step when funding becomes the obstacle.”

## The best bank line

> “If you have to tell a business owner no, I may be able to help them explore alternative options without damaging the relationship.”

## The best business owner line

> “The short application gives us enough information to see which funding paths may fit. If something makes sense, we’ll talk next steps. If not, we’ll be honest.”

## The best equipment/trucking/construction line

> “If the equipment, repair, payroll, or cash-flow gap is holding up revenue, let’s see what options may be available.”

---

# What Darwin Should Avoid Saying

Avoid these like expired gas station sushi:

| Avoid Saying | Better Alternative |
|---|---|
| “Everyone can get funded.” | “Options depend on the business profile.” |
| “We guarantee approvals.” | “We help explore possible funding paths.” |
| “Same-day funding for everyone.” | “Some options may move quickly depending on qualifications.” |
| “Banks are useless.” | “Banks have underwriting limits. We may be able to help when they cannot.” |
| “I can get you any amount.” | “The amount depends on revenue, credit, time in business, and provider criteria.” |
| “Bad credit doesn’t matter.” | “Credit is one factor, but not always the only factor.” |
| “Just fill this out and you’re approved.” | “The application starts the review process.” |
