## Purpose

This tracker helps Darwin measure the weekly activity that creates funding opportunities.

The goal is simple:

**More quality conversations → more referrals → more applications → more funding opportunities.**

This is not about chasing random people or sounding like a loan-broker carnival barker with a Bluetooth headset and questionable cologne.

This is about becoming a trusted commercial funding resource for:

- bank managers
- accountants
- attorneys
- equipment dealers
- trucking contacts
- construction-related businesses
- business owners with cash-flow problems, equipment needs, growth plans, or bank delays

# What Counts as a Quality Conversation?

A quality conversation is not just “I sent a link.”

A quality conversation means Darwin learned at least one useful thing:

- Who they serve
- What kind of businesses they know
- Whether their clients ever get declined, delayed, or underfunded
- Whether they see cash-flow problems
- Whether they would be open to referring business owners
- Whether the business owner has a real funding need
- Whether the timing is now, later, or not a fit

---

# Simple Conversation Prompts

Use these when logging or reviewing conversations.

## For Bank Managers

“What happens when a solid business client does not fit the bank’s box right now, but you still want to help them find a responsible next step?”

## For Accountants

“Do you ever have clients who are profitable on paper but tight on cash when taxes, payroll, inventory, or growth costs hit?”

## For Attorneys

“Do you work with business owners who need capital during formation, expansion, contract fulfillment, disputes, acquisitions, or major transitions?”

## For Equipment / Trucking / Construction Contacts

“Do you run into business owners who need equipment, repairs, materials, payroll, or job-start capital before the next check clears?”

## For Business Owners

“What are you trying to accomplish with funding — solve a short-term cash-flow gap, buy equipment, cover payroll, take on a job, or grow faster?”

---

# What To Avoid

Darwin should avoid saying or implying:

- “Everyone qualifies.”
- “Approval is guaranteed.”
- “Funding is guaranteed.”
- “You will get funded today.”
- “Credit does not matter.”
- “We approve what banks deny.”
- “This is free money.”
- “This will not affect your business.”
- “You can definitely get the amount you want.”
- “The bank failed you.”

Better language:

- “We can help explore options.”
- “Approval, terms, amount, and timing depend on the business profile.”
- “The application helps identify possible funding paths.”
- “Some businesses may qualify for faster options, while others may need more documentation.”
- “This can be a responsible next step when traditional financing is delayed or not a fit.”

# Weekly Review Questions

Complete these at the end of each week.

## Activity Review

1. How many referral partner conversations did I have?
2. How many business owner conversations did I have?
3. How many applications were started?
4. How many applications were completed or submitted?
5. How many follow-ups did I complete?
6. Which category produced the best conversations this week?

## Relationship Review

1. Which referral partner seems most likely to send a real referral?
2. Who needs a simple one-pager or follow-up message?
3. Who asked a question I need help answering better?
4. Which relationship should I deepen next week?

## Pipeline Review

1. Who is closest to submitting an application?
2. Who started but stalled?
3. Who needs a reminder, explanation, or simple next step?
4. Who is not a fit right now but may be worth checking on later?

## Learning Review

1. What worked this week?
2. What felt awkward or unclear?
3. What objection came up more than once?
4. What should I say differently next week?
5. What market or referral lane seems strongest?
