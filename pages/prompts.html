Launch Kit Prompts

Build Partner kits from source notes.

Use launch kit prompts to turn a Partner profile, referral lanes, audience notes, and resource references into a structured launch asset.

  • Define Partner lane and ideal referral sources.
  • Summarize safe positioning.
  • List recommended scripts, trackers, and handouts.
  • Include provider criteria and no-guarantee language.

Referral Asset Prompts

Turn audience context into useful assets.

Generate first drafts for bank manager handouts, accountant intros, attorney referral copy, equipment dealer notes, and local connector scripts.

  • Name the referral audience.
  • State the business problem they commonly see.
  • Use “explore funding options” language.
  • Avoid approval, rate, amount, and timeline claims.

Script-Writing Prompts

Prompt the script, then inspect the claims.

Script prompts should produce clean draft copy for conversations, emails, follow-ups, and objection handling. Review every funding-related statement before use.

Conversation Prompt

Draft discovery questions and safe next-step language for business owner conversations.

Email Prompt

Draft warm introductions, referral follow-ups, and business owner recap emails.

Objection Prompt

Draft safe answers to questions about rates, timing, documentation, approval, and provider review.

Compliance Rewrite Prompts

Make risky copy boring enough to survive.

Compliance rewrite prompts should identify unsafe claims, rewrite them with approved language, and explain why the rewrite is safer.

  • Flag guaranteed approval language.
  • Remove rate, term, amount, and timeline promises.
  • Add provider criteria language.
  • Use the default disclaimer when funding options are discussed.

Prompt Rule

Never ask AI to make funding claims more persuasive. Ask it to make the copy clearer, safer, and easier to review.

Email Copy Prompt

Draft short email copy for Partner follow-up, referral sources, and asset delivery.

Prompt usage rules

  • Tell the model to avoid funding guarantees.
  • Include approved language in the prompt.
  • Ask for compliance notes with every funding-related draft.
  • Review output before publishing.

Do not prompt for

  • Guaranteed approval claims
  • Income or revenue promises
  • Specific rates or terms
  • Funding amount promises
  • Urgency that implies guaranteed timing
