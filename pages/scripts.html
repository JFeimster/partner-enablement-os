Referral Partner Scripts

Referral sources need trust-first language.

The best referral scripts do not make the Partner sound like a walking banner ad. They help trusted professionals introduce business owners to a responsible next step.

  • Use “explore funding options” instead of “get approved.”
  • Use “may be available” instead of “you qualify.”
  • Use “provider criteria apply” before discussing possible next steps.
  • Use “when traditional financing is delayed, unavailable, or not the right fit today” for banker-friendly framing.

Reminder

Referral scripts protect the relationship first. No approval promises. No timeline promises. No “everyone qualifies” nonsense.

Objection Handling

Answer questions without inventing facts.

If a Partner does not know the answer, the correct move is to say what can be reviewed, not to improvise terms like a caffeinated fortune teller.

“What rate can I get?”

“Possible terms depend on the business profile and provider criteria. The review process helps identify options that may be available.”

“How fast can I get funded?”

“Timing is not guaranteed. It depends on provider review, documentation, business profile, and the available options.”

“Am I approved?”

“Approval is not guaranteed. The next step is to review the business profile and see what possible funding paths may be available.”

“Do I need documents?”

“Documentation requirements vary by provider and business profile. Provider criteria apply.”

Copy/Paste Guidance

Copy scripts. Do not copy bad assumptions.

Before using a script, adjust the audience, context, and next step. Keep the safe language intact.

Before sending

  • Confirm the audience.
  • Remove any unsupported claims.
  • Add provider criteria language.
  • Include the default disclaimer when funding options are discussed.

Never add

  • Guaranteed approval
  • Specific rate promises
  • Specific amount promises
  • Timeline guarantees
  • Universal qualification claims
