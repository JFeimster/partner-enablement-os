Daily Activity Trackers

Daily actions keep the Partner engine warm.

The Daily 5 Tracker is the simplest starting point: five useful actions each day, logged clearly enough to review later.

Daily 5 Tracker

Track five useful actions: introductions, follow-ups, asset shares, business owner conversations, or CRM updates.

Partner Outreach Log

Track who was contacted, why, what was sent, and the next follow-up date.

Follow-Up Queue

Keep open conversations from turning into archaeological artifacts.

Weekly Review Templates

Review the week before the week reviews you.

Weekly reviews help Partners identify what worked, what stalled, what needs follow-up, and where more context is needed.

Partner Weekly Review

Use a weekly review to summarize outreach, referral conversations, follow-ups, resource usage, stuck opportunities, and next-week priorities.

  • How many useful Partner actions were completed?
  • Which referral sources need follow-up?
  • Which business owner conversations need more information?
  • Which assets or scripts were useful?
  • What is the next action for each open opportunity?

Reminder

Review activity and next steps. Do not treat tracker data as a promise of approval, funding, revenue, income, or outcomes.

Referral Outreach Tracker

Referral relationships need follow-up discipline.

Bank managers, accountants, attorneys, dealers, trucking contacts, construction contacts, and local connectors should be tracked by relationship, context, and next action.

Referral Source

Name, role, company, audience, and relationship context.

Conversation Notes

What they care about, who they serve, and what business problems they see.

Assets Shared

Track handouts, scripts, explainers, emails, and next-step links.

Next Follow-Up

Date, channel, reason, and owner. Follow-up without context is just noise with a calendar invite.

Application & Follow-Up Tracker

Track status without promising results.

Application and follow-up trackers should organize information, documentation, and next steps. They should not imply approval, funding amounts, terms, rates, or timing.

Track this

  • Business owner name and company
  • Use of funds and timing context
  • Documents requested or received
  • Provider review status
  • Next action and owner

Do not claim this

  • Approved unless provider confirms
  • Guaranteed amount
  • Guaranteed timeline
  • Guaranteed rate or term
  • Universal qualification

Keep It Simple

Do not build a CRM cathedral for a five-action problem.

Start with daily actions, weekly review, referral outreach, and follow-up status. Complexity is earned after usage proves it needs to exist.

Start small

Use the smallest tracker that creates useful follow-up.

Review often

A tracker nobody reviews is just a spreadsheet wearing a costume.

Improve monthly

Add fields only when the work proves they are needed.
