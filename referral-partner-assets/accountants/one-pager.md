# Three Common Client Situations

## The Tax Bill Is Due. The Cash Isn’t.

A client may be profitable on paper and still be gasping for cash. Payroll, taxes, vendors, receivables, and operating costs may be squeezing the bank account.

**Best capital angle:** Fast Working Capital, Business Line Access, Invoice & Receivables Funding, or Larger Funding Reviews.

## The Client Is Growing — But Cash Flow Is Dragging Behind.

More orders, payroll, inventory, equipment, and project costs can break a business almost as fast as failure.

**Best capital angle:** Business Line Access, Larger Funding Reviews, Equipment Financing, Structured Growth Funding, or Invoice & Receivables Funding.

## The Client Did the Work. The Money Hasn’t Landed.

Receivables, progress payments, customer invoices, or Net-30 / Net-60 / Net-90 terms can trap money after the work is done.

**Best capital angle:** Invoice & Receivables Funding, Fast Working Capital, or Business Line Access.
