# Three Common Client Situations

## The Entity Is Formed. The Business Still Needs Capital.

A client can have clean documents, a clear plan, and a newly formed entity — but no money to launch, operate, market, hire, or buy what the business needs.

**Best capital angle:** Startup Funding Path, Fast Working Capital, Business Line Access, or Larger Funding Reviews.

## The Deal Is Ready. Capital Is Missing.

A buyout, acquisition, expansion, contract, or restructuring can stall when the client needs capital to close, mobilize, or stabilize the business.

**Best capital angle:** Larger Funding Reviews, Structured Growth Funding, Equipment Financing, Real Estate Capital Track, or Working Capital.

## The Client Has a Business Problem With a Funding Gap Inside It.

Litigation pressure, delayed payments, equipment needs, payroll gaps, contract startup costs, or vendor obligations can turn a legal/business issue into a cash-flow problem.

**Best capital angle:** Fast Working Capital, Invoice & Receivables Funding, Business Line Access, or Equipment Financing.
