# Referral Partner Objection Handling Scripts

**Asset Type:** Canonical script  
**Audience:** Referral partners, professional contacts, business connectors  
**Partner-Specific:** No  

---

## Purpose

Use these responses when a referral source has concerns about sending business owners to a funding review.

Keep the tone respectful, calm, and practical.

---

## Objection: “I do not want to risk my relationship.”

I completely understand. That relationship matters.

That is why I keep the conversation professional, set realistic expectations, and never promise approval, amounts, terms, or timelines.

The goal is not to pressure the business owner. The goal is to give them a practical next step when funding is the bottleneck.

---

## Objection: “I do not want to sell loans.”

You do not have to sell anything.

You are simply pointing someone to a funding review resource when they already have a capital problem.

I handle the funding conversation. You stay focused on your relationship and your core business.

---

## Objection: “What if they do not qualify?”

That can happen.

The application helps identify whether possible funding paths exist based on the business profile. If it is not a fit, expectations should stay clear.

A responsible no is still better than sending someone into random online loan chaos.

---

## Objection: “My clients usually go to banks.”

That makes sense. Banks are still an important first option for many business owners.

I am most useful when the bank is delayed, unavailable, not the right fit today, or when the business owner needs to explore other possible paths.

This is a next-step resource, not a replacement for strong banking relationships.

---

## Objection: “I do not know who to refer.”

Think about business owners who say things like:

- “I need working capital.”
- “Payroll is tight.”
- “The bank said no.”
- “I need equipment.”
- “I am waiting on invoices.”
- “I have the job, but I need cash to start.”
- “I need money fast.”

Those are the conversations where a funding review may make sense.

---

## Objection: “Is this going to spam my client?”

No. The goal is a professional funding review, not a spam circus.

The business owner completes an application, their profile is reviewed, and possible next steps are discussed based on available paths and provider criteria.

---

## Objection: “What do I say when introducing you?”

Use this:

```text
I know someone who helps business owners explore funding options when working capital, equipment, payroll, invoices, repairs, startup costs, or bank delays create pressure. I cannot promise an outcome, but he can help you review possible next steps.
```

---

## Footer

Funding is subject to application, review, provider criteria, and business profile. Approval, amount, terms, cost, and timing are not guaranteed.
